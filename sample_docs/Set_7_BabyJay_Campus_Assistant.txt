Hey, I'm Pavan. I built BabyJay — a campus assistant for KU, live at babyjay.bot.

KU has over 3,000 websites. If you want office hours, building hours, or the next bus, that's three sites and three searches. Ask ChatGPT and it makes things up. The info exists, it's just scattered.

I wanted one place students could ask, with answers grounded in real KU sources, and cheap enough to keep current.

I looked at a few options. An FAQ — too rigid, students don't ask cleanly. Fine-tuning — expensive, and stale the moment KU updates a page. So I went with RAG: cheap to update, and every answer cites a real source.

I scraped KU's official sites into structured JSON. When a student asks something, I pull the relevant chunks and hand them to Claude with strict rules — only use what I give you, and cite it.

The piece I'm proud of is the routing. Naive RAG runs vector search on every query — slow, and it guesses intent. I added a classifier that reads the question first. "Tell me about Dr. David Johnson" — it knows that's a professor, hits the faculty table, returns in under 5 milliseconds. "What's the intro programming course" — that's semantic, so it runs vector and keyword search, merges them, and a cross-encoder re-ranks. About 80% of queries skip the slow path entirely.

Stack: FastAPI, ChromaDB, Supabase, React on Vercel. Nine custom scrapers. Pytest catches regressions when sources refresh.

It's sitting at 82% thumbs-up, and that feedback flows into the eval set, so it gets sharper with every use.

Honestly, the AI wasn't the hard part — Claude does the heavy lifting. The hard part was the routing and the data pipeline.
